Ava Adore

A significant experience can force you to rethink major decisions you've settled upon. Consider deciding to break up with your girlfriend. You turn it over thoroughly and make your mind up.

You were never the strong one, so you decide to do it while she is out of town. And so you leave a note on her fridge, pack up your things, and move back into your place.

You go to bed that night and for some inexplicable reason, you pee on it. This makes you doubt your ability to fully trust in yourself. You are not reliable. You begin to rethink the breakup. You should at least push it by a few days, only till you have achieved clarity.

You hurry back to hers and replace your things. Then you snatch up the handwritten note you’d pasted on the fridge for her.

Something withered.
I no longer desired you in that capacity.
I didn't think it would be fair, or healthy—for the sake of your dignity—to persist in such a manner.

You crumple it, and chuck it in the bin. Silly you.

And you leave another, if only temporary one:

We must never be apart.
